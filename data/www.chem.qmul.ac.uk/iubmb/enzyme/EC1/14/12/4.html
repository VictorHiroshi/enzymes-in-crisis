IUBMB Enzyme Nomenclature

EC 1.14.12.4

Accepted name: 3-hydroxy-2-methylpyridinecarboxylate dioxygenase

Reaction: 3-hydroxy-2-methylpyridine-5-carboxylate + NAD(P)H + H+ + O2 = 2-(acetamidomethylene)succinate + NAD(P)+

Other name(s): methylhydroxypyridinecarboxylate oxidase; 2-methyl-3-hydroxypyridine 5-carboxylic acid dioxygenase; methylhydroxypyridine carboxylate dioxygenase; 3-hydroxy-3-methylpyridinecarboxylate dioxygenase [incorrect]; 3-hydroxy-2-methylpyridine-5-carboxylate,NAD(P)H:oxygen oxidoreductase (decyclizing)

Systematic name: 3-hydroxy-2-methylpyridine-5-carboxylate,NAD(P)H:oxygen oxidoreductase (ring-opening)

Comments: A flavoprotein (FAD).

Links to other databases: BRENDA, EXPASY, KEGG, Metacyc, UM-BBD, CAS registry number: 37256-69-2

References:

1. Sparrow, L.G., Ho, P.P.K., Sundaram, T.K., Zach, D., Nyns, E.J. and Snell, E.E. The bacterial oxidation of vitamin B6. VII. Purification, properties, and mechanism of action of an oxygenase which cleaves the 3-hydroxypyridine ring. J. Biol. Chem. 244 (1969) 2590-2600. [PMID: 4306031]

[EC 1.14.12.4 created 1972]
